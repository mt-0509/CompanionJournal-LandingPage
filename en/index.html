What is a 3-line journal?

By fitting your day into just three short prompts—like “something that went well,” “something on your mind,” and “one free line”—you can capture movement in your feelings without over-organizing it. A simple habit that leaves just enough space.

  • Easy to keep

    Designed to be done in 60 seconds. No perfection—just small accumulation.

  • Patterns become visible

    Your daily lines line up over time, revealing mood waves and perspective habits.

  • Room to breathe

    Because you don’t write too much, it’s easier for AI to support reflection later.

AI insights that “stay gentle”

Companion Journal’s AI isn’t here to judge or label you—it’s a companion that offers prompts for noticing. It summarizes soft tendencies from your words and returns perspectives that can nourish your mind.

Emotion analysis

Estimates your primary emotional tone from your phrasing. Helps you notice recurring emotional waves.

Positive/negative balance

Visualizes the balance of upbeat vs. negative expressions so you can reflect: “What lens did I have this week?”

Habit awareness

Highlights repeated actions, people, and places. Your energy sources and stressors become clearer.

Example of a supportive message

“This week, the word ‘tired’ appears a bit more often. If you can reserve just one small window to rest, recovery may come easier. You wrote ‘it was fun’ about taking a walk last week—could you fit in another short walk?”

Gentle features to help you continue

Designed to never blame “days you couldn’t write,” and to quietly stack “days you did.” We lowered the barrier to making it a habit.

A UI that’s easy to write in

Only three input boxes. Light guidance on length, and an “if you wrote it, it counts” feel.

Push notifications

A soft reminder like: “Want to put today into words?”

Calendar view

Watching days fill in creates small wins. Color-coding emotions helps you see your flow.

Your self accumulates

A timeline that increases clarity when you reread: “Oh, that’s how I felt back then.”
